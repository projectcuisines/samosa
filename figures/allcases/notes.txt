Standard Deviation Figure:

  Spread metric                                                                                                                                                                                                     
  Each panel shows the variance-weighted inter-model standard deviation across the full (instellation, pN₂) parameter space. For surface temperature the std is in K; for water vapor it is in dex (σ of log₁₀ of   
  the column in kg m⁻²); for cloud fraction it is in %.                                                                                                                                                             
                                                            
  Kriging                                                                                                                                                                                                           
  Each model's data were independently interpolated onto the common grid using Ordinary Kriging with a linear variogram, with axes normalized to [0,1] before fitting. Water vapor was kriged in natural-log space;
  cloud fraction in logit space (then back-transformed via sigmoid to % before computing the spread).                                                                                                               
                                                            
  Runaway exclusion                                                                                                                                                                                                 
  Cases flagged as runaway greenhouse (sentinel values: 600 K for temperature, 10⁴ kg m⁻² for water vapor, 200% for cloud fraction) were excluded from kriging for all variables. PlaHab provides no water vapor
  data and is excluded from that panel only.                                                                                                                                                                        
  
  Variance weighting                                                                                                                                                                                                
  Rather than a simple standard deviation, each model's contribution is weighted by the inverse of its kriging variance (w_i = 1/σ²_i), so models that are interpolating near actual sample points carry more weight
   than those extrapolating into unsampled regions. To prevent sample points from receiving arbitrarily large weight (kriging variance is exactly zero at exact data locations with exact_values=True), the variance
   floor is capped at 10% of the median kriging variance across all models and grid points, bounding the maximum weight ratio at approximately 10:1.
                                                                                                                                                                                                                    
  Single-model masking                                      
  Hatched regions indicate areas where only one model (ExoPlaSim) has valid sample data — specifically the three QMC sample points at (1900 W m⁻², 7.85 bar), (2400 W m⁻², 0.21 bar), and (2100 W m⁻², 1.83 bar),
  which are runaway cases for ExoCAM, ROCKE-3D, and PlaHab and outside the sampling subsets of Generic PCM and LFRic. The boundary was determined by kriging the per-point model count and masking where the        
  interpolated count falls below 1.5. Sample points are shown as open circles (multiple models) or × marks (single model only).
